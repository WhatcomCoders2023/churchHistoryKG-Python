<h2>Key Developments</h2>
<!--- A bulleted list of important developments during that period.-->

<ul>
<li>Observance of the Sabbath moved to Sundays in remembrance of Jesus' resurrection from the dead.</li>
<li>Unbaptized believers were prepared for baptism through catechumen training.</li>
<li>The Eucharist was practiced by baptized Christians, and often accompanied by the love feast, a communal meal shared by believers. </li>
<li>Christian holy days were celebrated.</li>
<li>Liturgical writings were developed to instruct leaders on the order of worship as early Christian practices became more uniform.</li>
</ul>

<h2>Key Concepts</h2>
<ul>
<li>The <a href="https://ref.ly/logos4/Factbook?ref=bk.%25epiclesis">epiclesis</a> developed as a prayer to consecrate the breed and wine in the Eucharist.</li>
<li>The <a href="https://ref.ly/logos4/Factbook?ref=bk.%25LordsSupper">Lord’s Supper</a> was at the center of early Christian worship.</li>
<li><a href="https://ref.ly/logos4/Factbook?ref=bk.%25LoveFeast">Love Feasts</a> were shared among believers in the early church, at times in conjunction with the Eucharist.</li>
</ul>

<h3>Church Orders</h3>
<ul>
<li><a href="https://ref.ly/logos4/Factbook?ref=bk.%25Didache_Writing"><em>Didache</em></a> (1st century): manual on early Christian practices and ethics; includes instructions on baptism, the Eucharist, prayer and fasting, and more.</li>
<li><em>Apostolic Traditions</em> (Hippolytus of Rome, c. 200): addresses early church liturgy, organization, and ethics; includes a liturgy for the Eucharist.</li>
<li><a href="https://ref.ly/logos4/Factbook?ref=bk.%25ApostolicConstitutionsAndCanons_Writing"><em>Apostolic Constitutions and Canons</em></a> (c. 350–380): manual on early Christian practices and ethics that originated from Syria; includes two collections, the <em>Apostolic Constitutions</em> and the <em>Apostolic Canons</em>.</li>
</ul>
