<h1>The Church of England Breaks with Rome</h1>
<ul>
<li><strong>Period:</strong> !!smallcaps|ad!! <a href="date.1521-1603">1521–1603</a></li>
<li><strong>Description:</strong> England separated from Rome and established the ruling monarch as head of the Church of England. </li>
</ul>
<h2>Summary</h2>
<p>The English Reformation was both a political and religious movement. The initial challenge to Rome was not in opposition to the church's doctrine, but to the authority of the pope. Henry VIII's pursuit of an annulment from his marriage to Catherine of Aragon, which had not resulted in a male heir, was denied by the pope and led to an intense political confrontation with Rome. The king eventually declared himself the supreme authority over the Church of England, cutting ties to the church in Rome. In the decades that followed, both Protestants and Catholics attempted to shape the newly independent Church of England. A compromise was finally reached during the reign of Elizabeth I, a blend of Roman Catholicism and Protestantism which defined the Anglican Church. </p>
<h2>Key Developments</h2>
<!--- A bulleted list of important developments during that period.-->

<ul>
<li>English Reformers challenged <a href="https://ref.ly/logos4/Factbook?ref=bk.%25PapalPrimacy_Writing">papal authority</a> and called for <a href="https://ref.ly/logos4/Factbook?ref=bk.%25reform">reform</a> in the church (<a href="date.1500+100">1500s</a>).</li>
<li><a href="https://ref.ly/logos4/Factbook?ref=bk.%25Protestantism_Belief">Protestants</a> advocated for English translations of the <a href="https://ref.ly/logos4/Factbook?ref=bk.%25Bible_Writing">Bible</a> (<a href="date.1500+100">1500s</a>).</li>
<li><a href="https://ref.ly/logos4/Factbook?ref=bk.%25monastery">Monasteries</a> were dissolved in <a href="https://ref.ly/logos4/Factbook?ref=bk.%40England">England</a> (<a href="date.1530+10">1530s</a>).</li>
<li><a href="https://ref.ly/logos4/Factbook?ref=bio.HenryVIII">Henry VIII</a> rejected papal authority and became the head of the <a href="https://ref.ly/logos4/Factbook?ref=bk.%25ChurchOfEngland">Church of England</a> while reaffirming the doctrine of the <a href="https://ref.ly/logos4/Factbook?ref=bk.%25RomanCatholicism_Belief">Roman Catholic Church</a> (<a href="date.1534">1534</a>).</li>
<li><a href="https://ref.ly/logos4/Factbook?ref=bk.%25KingEdwardVIEngland_Person">Edward VI</a> advanced the <a href="https://ref.ly/logos4/Factbook?ref=bk.tle%3aprotestantreformation">Protestant Reformation</a> in England (<a href="date.1547-1553">1547–1553</a>).</li>
<li>The <a href="https://ref.ly/logos4/Factbook?ref=bk.%25BookOfCommonPrayer_Writing"><em>Book of Common Prayer</em></a> replaced Latin worship in the Church of England (<a href="date.1549">1549</a>).</li>
<li>Protestants were persecuted under the Catholic queen <a href="https://ref.ly/logos4/Factbook?ref=bk.%25MaryTudor_Person">Mary Tudor</a>, and many were forced into exile (<a href="date.1553-1558">1553–1558</a>).</li>
<li>The <a href="bk.%ElizabethanSettlement">Elizabethan Settlement</a> offered a compromise between Catholicism and Protestantism; <a href="https://ref.ly/logos4/Factbook?ref=bk.%25Anglicanism_Belief">Anglicanism</a> was established (<a href="date.1559">1559</a>).</li>
</ul>

<h3><a href="bk.%King">Monarchs</a></h3>
<ul>
<li><a href="bio.CatherineOfAragon">Catherine of Aragon</a> (<a href="date.1485-1536.1.7">1485–January 7, 1536</a>): Spanish <a href="bk.#Queen">queen</a> of <a href="https://ref.ly/logos4/Factbook?ref=bk.%40England">England</a>; first wife of Henry VIII; her marriage to Henry was annulled as a part of England's break with <a href="https://ref.ly/logos4/Factbook?ref=bk.%25Rome_Place">Rome</a>. </li>
<li><a href="https://ref.ly/logos4/Factbook?ref=bio.HenryVIII">Henry VIII</a> (<a href="date.1491.6.28-1547.1.28">June 28, 1491–January 28, 1547</a>): <a href="bk.%King">king</a> of England (<a href="date.1509-1547">1509-1547</a>); broke with the <a href="https://ref.ly/logos4/Factbook?ref=bk.%25RomanCatholicism_Belief">Roman Catholic Church</a> and rejected the authority of the <a href="https://ref.ly/logos4/Factbook?ref=bk.%25pope">pope</a>; issued the <a href="bk.%SupremacyCrownAct1534_Writing">Supremacy of the Crown Act</a>, which declared him, as the English monarch, the "Supreme Head of the Church of England"; issued the <a href="bk.%SixArticles_Writing">Six Articles</a>, which affirmed key Catholic doctrine.</li>
<li><a href="https://ref.ly/logos4/Factbook?ref=bk.%25AnneBoleyn_Person">Anne Boleyn</a> (<a href="date.1501+c-1536.5.19">c. 1501–May 19, 1536</a>): queen of England (<a href="date.1533-1536">1533–1536</a>; second wife of Henry VIII; mother of Elizabeth I; executed for treason on accusations of adultery.</li>
<li><a href="https://ref.ly/logos4/Factbook?ref=bk.%25KingEdwardVIEngland_Person">Edward VI</a> (<a href="date.1537.10.12-1553.7.6">October 12, 1537-July 6, 1553</a>): king of England (<a href="date.1547-1553">1547–1553</a>); he was influenced by <a href="https://ref.ly/logos4/Factbook?ref=bk.%25ThomasCranmer_Person">Thomas Cranmer</a> and pushed for reform in the English church; repealed the Six Articles.</li>
<li><a href="https://ref.ly/logos4/Factbook?ref=bk.%25MaryTudor_Person">Mary Tudor</a> (<a href="date.1516.2.18-1558.11.17">February 18, 1516–November 17, 1558</a>): Catholic <a href="https://ref.ly/logos4/Factbook?ref=bk.%queen">queen</a> of England (<a href="date.1553-1558">1553--1558</a>); she issued the <a href="bk.%SeeOfRomeAct_Writing">See of Rome Act</a> reestablishing the pope as the head of the church in England; commissioned the use of <a href="https://ref.ly/logos4/Factbook?ref=bk.%25heresy">heresy</a> trials to root out Reformers and came to be known as "Bloody Mary" by Protestants. </li>
<li><a href="https://ref.ly/logos4/Factbook?ref=bk.%25QueenElizabethIEngland_Person">Elizabeth I</a> (<a href="date.1533.9.7-1603.3.24">September 7, 1533–March 24, 1603</a>): Protestant queen of England (<a href="date.1558-1603">1558-1603</a>; established the <a href="bk.%ElizabethanSettlement">Elizabethan Settlement</a>, a compromise between Roman Catholicism and Protestantism, which shaped the <a href="https://ref.ly/logos4/Factbook?ref=bk.%25ChurchOfEngland">Church of England</a>.</li>
</ul>

<h3><a href="bk.%protestantreformer">Reformers</a></h3>
<ul>
<li><a href="https://ref.ly/logos4/Factbook?ref=bk.%25HughLatimer_Person">Hugh Latimer</a> (<a href="date.1485-1555.10.15">1485–October 15, 1555</a>): English Reformer and <a href="https://ref.ly/logos4/Factbook?ref=bk.%martyr">martyr</a>; bishop of <a href="https://ref.ly/logos4/Factbook?ref=bk.%25Worcester">Worcester</a>; chaplain to <a href="https://ref.ly/logos4/Factbook?ref=bk.%25KingEdwardVIEngland_Person">Edward VI</a>; studied at <a href="https://ref.ly/logos4/Factbook?ref=bk.%25universityOfCambridge_Place">University of Cambridge</a>; he was executed under Mary Tudor.</li>

<li><a href="https://ref.ly/logos4/Factbook?ref=bk.%25MilesCoverdale_Person">Miles Coverdale</a> (<a href="date.1488-1569.1.20">1488–January 20, 1569</a>): English Reformer, <a href="https://ref.ly/logos4/Factbook?ref=bk.%25monkReligiousBrother">monk</a>, and Bible translator; he studied at Cambridge; he translated the <a href="bk.%CoverdaleBible_Writing">Coverdale Bible</a> and helped produce the <a href="bk.%GreatBible_Writing">Great Bible</a>.</li>
<li><a href="https://ref.ly/logos4/Factbook?ref=bk.%25ThomasCranmer_Person">Thomas Cranmer</a> (<a href="date.1489.7.2-1556.3.21">July 2, 1489–March 21, 1556</a>): English Reformer and martyr; he was the first Protestant <a href="https://ref.ly/logos4/Factbook?ref=bk.%25archbishop">archbishop</a> of <a href="https://ref.ly/logos4/Factbook?ref=bk.%25Canterbury">Canterbury</a>; studied at Cambridge; author of the <a href="bk.%BookOfCommonPrayer_Writing"><em>Book of Common Prayer</em></a>; developed the <a href="bk.%ThirtyNineArticles_Writing">Thirty-Nine Articles</a>; executed for treason and heresy under Mary Tudor.</li>
<li><a href="https://ref.ly/logos4/Factbook?ref=bk.%25WilliamTyndale_Person">William Tyndale</a> (<a href="date.1494-1536">1494–1536</a>): English Reformer and martyr; he studied at Cambridge and <a href="https://ref.ly/logos4/Factbook?ref=bk.%25Oxford">Oxford University</a>; translated the Bible into English; executed for heresy under Henry VIII.</li>
<li><a href="https://ref.ly/logos4/Factbook?ref=bio.JohnHooper">John Hooper</a> (<a href="date.1495+c-1555.2.9">c. 1495–February 9, 1555</a>): English Reformer and martyr; bishop of <a href="https://ref.ly/logos4/Factbook?ref=bk.%25Gloucester_Place">Gloucester</a> and Worcester; he studied at Oxford; executed under Mary Tudor.</li>
<li><a href="https://ref.ly/logos4/Factbook?ref=bio.NicholasRidley.1">Nicholas Ridley</a> (<a href="date.1500+c-1555.10.16">c. 1500–October 16, 1555</a>):  English Reformer and martyr; bishop of <a href="https://ref.ly/logos4/Factbook?ref=bk.%25London_Place">London</a>; chaplain to Thomas Cranmer and Henry VIII; he studied at Cambridge; executed under Mary Tudor.</li>
<li><a href="https://ref.ly/logos4/Factbook?ref=bk.%25MatthewParker_Person">Matthew Parker</a> (<a href="date.1504.8.6-1575.5.17">August 6, 1504–May 17, 1575</a>): English Reformer; archbishop of Canterbury; he helped develop the Thirty-Nine Articles and the <a href="bk.%BishopsBible_Writing">Bishops' Bible</a>; assisted with the implementation of the Elizabethan Settlement.</li>
</ul>

<h2>Key Concepts</h2>
<ul>
<li><a href="https://ref.ly/logos4/Factbook?ref=bk.%25Anglicanism_Belief">Anglicanism</a> developed as a compromise that combined elements of Protestantism and Roman Catholicism.</li>
<li><a href="https://ref.ly/logos4/Factbook?ref=bk.%25ChurchOfEngland">Church of England</a> separated from the <a href="https://ref.ly/logos4/Factbook?ref=bk.%25RomanCatholicism_Belief">Roman Catholic Church</a> during the reign of Henry VIII.</li>
<li><a href="https://ref.ly/logos4/Factbook?ref=bk.%25DefenderOfTheFaith">"Defender of the Faith"</a> was the title given to Henry VIII by <a href="https://ref.ly/logos4/Factbook?ref=bk.%25LeoX_Person">Pope Leo X</a> after he defended the Catholic <a href="https://ref.ly/logos4/Factbook?ref=bk.%25sacraments">sacraments</a> against <a href="https://ref.ly/logos4/Factbook?ref=bio.MartinLuther">Martin Luther</a>.</li>

<li><a href="https://ref.ly/logos4/Factbook?ref=bk.%25SubmissionoftheClergy">Submission of the Clergy</a>  was established by Henry VIII, requiring the Church of England's clergy to submit to the ecclesiastical authority of the English monarch.</li>
</ul>

<h3><a href="bk.%law">Legislative Acts</a></h3>
<ul>
<li><a href="bk.%StatuteInRestraintOfAppeals_Writing">Statute in Restraint of Appeals</a> (Henry VIII, <a href="date.1532">1532</a>): drafted by Thomas Cromwell; forbids appeals to the pope and established the English monarch the supreme authority on all matters related to England.</li>
<li><a href="bk.%SupremacyCrownAct1534_Writing">Supremacy of the Crown Act</a> (Henry VIII, <a href="date.1534">1534</a>): asserts the authority of the English monarch over the Church of England.</li>
<li><a href="bk.%SeeOfRomeAct_Writing">See of Rome Act</a> (Mary Tudor, <a href="date.1554">1554</a>): repeals Henry's Act of Supremacy and reestablishes the pope as the head of the church in England.</li>
<li><a href="bk.%ActOfSupremacy1558_Writing">Act of Supremacy 1558</a> (Elizabeth I): restores a revised version of Henry's Supremacy of the Crown Act reestablishing the English monarch's responsibility over the church.</li>
</ul>
<h3><a href="https://ref.ly/logos4/Factbook?ref=bk.%25liturgy">Liturgy</a></h3>
<ul>
<li><a href="https://ref.ly/logos4/Factbook?ref=bk.%25BookOfCommonPrayer_Writing"><em>Book of Common Prayer</em></a> (Thomas Cranmer, <a href="date.1548">1548</a>): revised by Edward VI (<a href="date.1552">1552</a>) and Elizabeth I (<a href="date.1559">1559</a>); the official liturgical book of Anglican churches.</li>
</ul>
<h3><a href="https://ref.ly/logos4/Factbook?ref=bk.%25papalBull">Papal Bulls</a></h3>
<ul>
<li><a href="https://ref.ly/logos4/Factbook?ref=bk.%25RegnansinExcelsis_Writing"><em>Regnans in Excelsis</em></a> (Pope Pius V, <a href="date.1570">1570</a>): papal bull excommunicating Elizabeth I.</li>
</ul>
<h3><a href="bk.%treatise">Treatises</a></h3>
<ul>
<li><a href="bk.%DeVeraObedientia_Writing"><em>De Vera Obedientia</em></a> (Stephen Gardiner, <a href="date.1535">1535</a>): argues for the supremacy of the monarch's authority over the church and rejects papal authority over the Church of England.</li>
</ul>
