<h1>The Roman Catholic Church Expands Beyond Europe</h1>
<ul>
<li><strong>Period:</strong> !!smallcaps|ad!! <a href="date.1480-1776">1480–1776</a></li>
<li><strong>Description:</strong> The Roman Catholic Church expanded through missionary efforts in areas outside of Europe during the Age of Exploration.</li>
</ul>
<h2>Summary</h2>
<p>While the Protestant Reformation was taking shape in Europe, the Roman Catholic Church was working to expand the borders of Christendom. During the Age of Discovery, Europeans embarked on overseas exploration in search of new travel routes and new lands. European monarchs, commissioned by the pope, sought to Christianize Europe's newly colonized territories. Religious orders, the dominant force in foreign missions during this period, traveled to the Americas to establish Roman Catholicism among its indigenous peoples. In the East, missionaries attempted to adapt Christianity to the culture but faced challenges from both those in the church and those opposed to Christianity. </p>

<h2>Key Concepts</h2>
<ul>
<li><a href="https://ref.ly/logos4/Factbook?ref=bk.%25Church">Churches</a> were established by foreign missionaries.</li>
<li><a href="https://ref.ly/logos4/Factbook?ref=bk.%25settle.verb">Colonization</a> was established as a means of propagating Christianity outside of Christendom.</li>
<li><a href="https://ref.ly/logos4/Factbook?ref=bk.%25evangelist">Missionaries</a> traveled to colonized territories beyond Christendom.</li>
<li><a href="https://ref.ly/logos4/Factbook?ref=bk.%25mission">Missions</a> was largely the work of monastic orders during the sixteenth--seventeenth centuries.</li>
<li><a href="https://ref.ly/logos4/Factbook?ref=bk.%25RomanCatholicism_Belief">Roman Catholicism</a> spread through colonization and missionary efforts to the Americas, Africa, and Asia.</li>
<li><a href="https://ref.ly/logos4/Factbook?ref=bk.%25sacredCongregationOfPropaganda">Sacred Congregation de Propaganda Fide</a> was founded by <a href="https://ref.ly/logos4/Factbook?ref=bk.%25PopeGregoryXV_Person">Pope Gregory XV</a> to promote the spread of Roman Catholicism and manage ecclesiastical issues in foreign territories.</li>
</ul>

<h3><a href="bk.%dictionariesAndEncyclopedias">Dictionaries and Encyclopedias</a></h3>
<ul>
<li><a href="bk.%FlorentineCodex-Writing"><em>General History of the Things of New Spain</em></a> (<a href="date.1501+100">Bernardino de Sahagún, sixteenth century</a>): encyclopedic work on the people and culture of Mexico; the information was compiled while de Sahagún was serving as a missionary in Mexico; also known as the Florentine Codex.</li>
</ul>
<h3><a href="https://ref.ly/logos4/Factbook?ref=bk.%25papalBull">Papal Bulls</a></h3>
<ul>
<li><a href="bk.%InterCaetera1493_Writing"><em>Inter Caetera</em></a> (Pope Alexander VI, <a href="date.1493">1493</a>): papal bull that divided up the lands outside of Christendom between the Roman Catholic monarchies of Spain and Portugal; established the Roman Catholic Church's support for European colonization as a means of propagating the Christian faith.</li>
<li><a href="bk.%SublimusDei_Writing"><em>Sublimis Deus</em></a> (<a href="https://ref.ly/logos4/Factbook?ref=bio.PopePaulIII">Pope Paul III</a>, <a href="date.1537">1537</a>): papal bull which addresses the enslavement and evangelization of the indigenous peoples of the Americas; states that indigenous peoples are fully rational human beings with the rights to freedom and private property even if they are not Christian converts. </li>
<li><a href="bk.%ImmensaPastorumPrincipis_Writing"><em>Immensa Pastorum Principis</em></a> (Pope Benedict XIV, <a href="date.1741">1741</a>): papal bull which addresses the enslavement and evangelization of the indigenous peoples of the Americas; the enslavement of indigenous peoples is condemned.</li>
<li><a href="bk.%ExQuoSingulari_Writing"><em>Ex Quo Singulari</em></a> (Pope Benedict XIV, <a href="date.1742">1742</a>): papal bull prohibiting some of the traditional practices the Jesuits had permitted among indigenous converts in China.</li>
<li><a href="bk.%OmniumSollicitudinum_Writing"><em>Omnium Sollicitudinum</em></a> (Pope Benedict XIV, <a href="date.1744">1744</a>): papal bull prohibiting some of the traditional practices the Jesuits had permitted among indigenous converts in India.</li>
</ul>
