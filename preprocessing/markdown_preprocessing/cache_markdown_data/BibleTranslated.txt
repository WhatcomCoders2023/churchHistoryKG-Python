<h1>The Christian Bible Is Translated Into Many Languages</h1>
<ul>
<li><strong>Period:</strong> !!smallcaps|ad!! <a href="date.382-1611">382-1611</a></li>
<li><strong>Description:</strong> Following the canonization of the New Testament, the Christian Bible was translated into other languages and became widely available with the invention of the printing press.</li>
</ul>
<h2>Summary</h2>
<p>The Septuagint, the Greek version of the Old Testament, was the Bible of the early church. Other writings, those of the apostles and other influential Christian leaders, were circulated among the churches. Soon the church began to collect these writings and establish the New Testament canon. As Latin prevailed in the Western church, the number of Latin translations in use grew. The translation of the Vulgate, originally based on the Greek Septuagint but later amended to reflect the Hebrew Bible, eventually became the authorized version of the Roman Catholic Church, and translation of the Bible into the vernacular was discouraged. The rise of humanism during the Renaissance and Reformation led to a renewed interest in the original Hebrew and Greek texts of the Bible and renewed demands for vernacular translations. As Protestantism spread across Europe and gained the support of monarchs, the Bible was translated into a variety of languages and became even more accessible with the invention of the printing press.</p>

<li>The first Bible was printed (<a href="date.1453-1456">1453–1456</a>).</li>
<li>The number of Bible translations grew rapidly despite facing opposition from state and church authorities <a href="date.1453-1500+100">1453–1500s</a>).</li>
<li>As <a href="https://ref.ly/logos4/Factbook?ref=bk.%25Protestantism_Belief">Protestantism</a> spread and was established as the state religion throughout Europe, Bible translations were commissioned by heads of state (<a href="date.1541-1611">1541-1611</a>).</li>
<li>The Vulgate became the official Bible of the <a href="https://ref.ly/logos4/Factbook?ref=bk.%25RomanCatholicism_Belief">Roman Catholic Church</a> (<a href="date.1546">1546</a>).</li>
</ul>

<h3><a href="https://ref.ly/logos4/Factbook?ref=bk.%25bibleTranslations">Bible Translators</a></h3>
<ul>
<li><a href="https://ref.ly/logos4/Factbook?ref=bk.%25Jerome_Person">Jerome</a> (<a href="date.347-420.9.30">347–September 30, 420</a>): <a href="bk.%LatinChurchFathers_People">Latin church father</a>; translator of the <a href="https://ref.ly/logos4/Factbook?ref=bk.%25Vulgate_Writing">Vulgate</a>, which became the official text of the <a href="https://ref.ly/logos4/Factbook?ref=bk.%25RomanCatholicism_Belief">Roman Catholic Church</a>.</li>
<li><a href="https://ref.ly/logos4/Factbook?ref=bk.%25SaintBedeTheVenerable_Person">Bede the Venerable</a> (<a href="date.672-735.5.26">672–May 26, 735</a>): English <a href="bk.%monkReligiousBrother">monk</a> and <a href="https://ref.ly/logos4/Factbook?ref=bk.%25scholar">scholar</a>; he translated the <a href="https://ref.ly/logos4/Factbook?ref=bk.%25GospelOfJohn_Writing">Gospel of John</a> into Old English.</li>
<li><a href="https://ref.ly/logos4/Factbook?ref=bk.%25JohnWycliffe_Person">John Wycliffe</a> (<a href="date.1330-1384">1330–1384</a>): English scholar and <a href="https://ref.ly/logos4/Factbook?ref=bk.%25theologian">theologian</a>; he advocated for, and possibly played some role in the translation of, an English vernacular version of the Bible which resulted in the <a href="https://ref.ly/logos4/Factbook?ref=bk.%25WyclifsVersion_Writing">Wycliffe Bible</a> (English); he was declared a <a href="https://ref.ly/logos4/Factbook?ref=bk.%25heretic">heretic</a> after his death.</li>
<li><a href="bio.niccolomalermi">Niccolò Malermi</a> (<a href="date.1422+c-1481">c. 1422–1481</a>): Italian biblical scholar; translator of the <a href="bk.%MalermiBible_Writing">Malermi Bible</a>, which was the first printed Italian translation.</li>
<li><a href="https://ref.ly/logos4/Factbook?ref=bk.%25JacobusFaber_Person">Jacques Lefèvre d’Étaples</a> (<a href="date.1455-1536">1455–1536</a>): French <a href="https://ref.ly/logos4/Factbook?ref=bk.%25Humanism_Belief">humanist</a>; he was the first to translate the Bible into French.</li>
<li><a href="https://ref.ly/logos4/Factbook?ref=bio.DesideriusErasmus">Desiderius Erasmus</a> (<a href="date.1466.10.27-1536.7.12">October 27, 1466–July 12, 1536</a>): <a href="https://ref.ly/logos4/Factbook?ref=bk.%25Dutch_People">Dutch</a> Roman Catholic theologian, scholar, and <a href="https://ref.ly/logos4/Factbook?ref=bk.%25Humanism_Belief">humanist</a>; he translated the <a href="https://ref.ly/logos4/Factbook?ref=bk.%25NewTestament_Writing">New Testament</a> into <a href="https://ref.ly/logos4/Factbook?ref=bk.%25LatinLanguage_Language">Latin</a> based off the <a href="https://ref.ly/logos4/Factbook?ref=bk.%25Vulgate_Writing">Vulgate</a> and included a collation of Greek texts which became known as the <a href="https://ref.ly/logos4/Factbook?ref=bk.%25TextusReceptus_Writing">Textus Receptus</a>.</li>
<li><a href="https://ref.ly/logos4/Factbook?ref=bio.MartinLuther">Martin Luther</a> (<a href="date.1483.11.10-1546.2.18">November 10, 1483–February 18, 1546</a>): German Protestant Reformer; translator of the <a href="bk.%LutherBible_Writing">Luther Bible</a> (German).</li>
<li><a href="https://ref.ly/logos4/Factbook?ref=bk.%25WilliamTyndale_Person">William Tyndale</a> (<a href="date.1494-1536.10.6">1494–October 6, 1536</a>): English Protestant Reformer; translator of the <a href="bk.%Tyndale_BibleTranslation">Tyndale Bible</a>, which was the first Bible printed in English; he was burned at the stake after being charged with <a href="https://ref.ly/logos4/Factbook?ref=bk.%25heresy">heresy</a>.</li>

<li><a href="https://ref.ly/logos4/Factbook?ref=bk.%25GiovanniDiodati_Person">Giovanni Diodati</a> (<a href="date.1576.6.6-1649.10.3">June 6, 1576–October 3, 1649</a>): Italian theologian; adhered to <a href="https://ref.ly/logos4/Factbook?ref=bk.%25Calvinism_Belief">Calvinism</a>; translator of <a href="bk.%GiovanniDiodatiBibbia_Writing">Diodati Bible</a>, which was used by Italian <a href="https://ref.ly/logos4/Factbook?ref=bk.%25Protestantism_Belief">Protestants</a>.</li>
</ul>

<h3><a href="https://ref.ly/logos4/Factbook?ref=bk.%25bibleTranslations">Bible Translations</a></h3>
<ul>
<li><a href="https://ref.ly/logos4/Factbook?ref=bk.%25Septuagint_Writing">Septuagint</a> (<a href="date.101+100">second century !!smallcaps|bc!!</a>): a Greek version of the Hebrew <a href="https://ref.ly/logos4/Factbook?ref=bk.%25OldTestament_Writing">Old Testament</a> that was used for many Bible translations.</li>
<li><a href="https://ref.ly/logos4/Factbook?ref=bk.%25Vulgate_Writing">Vulgate</a> (Jerome, <a href="date.383-405">383–405</a>): Latin translation of the Bible; became the official text of the Roman Catholic Church.</li>
<li><a href="https://ref.ly/logos4/Factbook?ref=bk.%25HarkleanVersion">Harclean Version</a> (<a href="bio.thomasofharkel">Thomas Harkel</a>, <a href="date.616">616</a>): <a href="https://ref.ly/logos4/Factbook?ref=bk.%25SyriacLanguage_Language">Syriac</a> translation of the New Testament; included in the <a href="https://ref.ly/logos4/Factbook?ref=bk.%25syriacVersions">Syriac Bible</a>.</li>
<li><a href="https://ref.ly/logos4/Factbook?ref=bk.%25WyclifsVersion_Writing">Wycliffe's Version </a> (John Wycliffe, <a href="date.1380-1384">1380–1384</a>): English vernacular translation based on the Latin Vulgate; used by English Catholics in the <a href="date.1501+100">sixteenth century</a>; it is unclear what Wycliffe's exact role in this translation was, whether he was responsible for translating, provided supervision, or merely inspired the translation.</li>
<li><a href="https://ref.ly/logos4/Factbook?ref=bk.%25MazarinBible_Object">Gutenberg Bible</a> (Johann Gutenberg, <a href="date.1453-1456">1453–1456</a>): first printed Bible using the Latin Vulgate.</li>
<li><a href="bk.%MentelinBible_Writing">Mentelin Bible</a> (Johann Mentelin, <a href="date.1466">1466</a>): first printed German Bible and first printed Bible in any language other than Latin.</li>
<li><a href="bk.%MalermiBible_Writing">Malermi Bible</a> (Niccolò Malermi, <a href="date.1471">1471</a>): first Italian printed Bible.</li>
<li><a href="bk.%LutherBible_Writing">Luther Bible</a> (Martin Luther, <a href="date.1522-1534">1522–1534</a>): German translation of the Bible based on <a href="https://ref.ly/logos4/Factbook?ref=bk.%25TextusReceptus_Writing">Textus Receptus</a> and <a href="https://ref.ly/logos4/Factbook?ref=bk.%25masoreticText">Masoretic Text</a>.</li>
<li><a href="bk.%Tyndale_BibleTranslation">Tyndale Bible</a> (William Tyndale, <a href="date.1525-1530">1525–1530</a>): English translation of the Pentateuch and New Testament based on Textus Receptus.</li>
<li><a href="bk.%GustavVasaBible_Writing">Gustav Vasa Bible</a> (<a href="bio.laurentiusandreae">Laurentius Andreae</a>, <a href="bio.laurentiuspetri">Laurentius Petri</a>, and <a href="bio.olauspetri">Olaus Petri</a>, <a href="date.1541">1541</a>): commissioned by Gustav I of Sweden; the first Swedish Bible printed.</li>
<li><a href="bk.%ChristianIIIBible_Writing">Christian III Bible</a> (<a href="date.1550">1550</a>): commissioned by Christian III of Denmark and Norway; the first Danish Bible printed.</li>
<li><a href="https://ref.ly/logos4/Factbook?ref=bk.%25GenevaBible_Writing">Geneva Bible</a> (<a href="date.1560">1560</a>): English translation of the Bible with Calvinist influences; translated by a team of English Protestant scholars who were in exile during the <a href="https://ref.ly/logos4/Factbook?ref=bk.tle%3areignmaryiengland">reign of Mary I</a>; it was the primary English Protestant Bible during the Reformation; the English rendering was dependent on the Tyndale Bible; the New Testament was dependent on the Textus Receptus; first English Bible to use verse numbers.</li>
<li><a href="https://ref.ly/logos4/Factbook?ref=bk.%25KingJamesVersion_Writing">King James Version</a> (<a href="date.1611">1611</a>): English version authorized by King James I based on Textus Receptus.</li>
</ul>
<h3><a href="https://ref.ly/logos4/Factbook?ref=bk.%25oldTestamentManuscripts">Old Testament Manuscripts</a></h3>
<ul>
<li><a href="https://ref.ly/logos4/Factbook?ref=bk.%25masoreticText">Masoretic Text</a> (<a href="date.600+c-950">c. 600–950</a>): traditional Hebrew text of the Old Testament preserved by the <a href="https://ref.ly/logos4/Factbook?ref=bk.%25Masoretes">Masoretes</a> that was used for many Bible translations.</li>
</ul>

<ul>
<li><a href="https://ref.ly/logos4/Factbook?ref=bk.%25TextusReceptus_Writing">Textus Receptus</a> (Desiderius Erasmus, <a href="date.1516">1516</a>): Greek text of the New Testament that was used for several Bible translations.</li>
</ul>
