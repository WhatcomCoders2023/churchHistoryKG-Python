<h1>The Church Organizes Under Bishops</h1>
<ul>
<li><strong>Period:</strong> !!smallcaps|ad!! 50–451</li>
<li><strong>Description:</strong> The early church developed under the care and leadership of bishops, elders, and deacons/deaconesses. </li>
</ul>
<h2>Key Developments</h2>
<!--- A bulleted list of important developments during that period.-->

<ul>
<li>The apostles Paul and Barnabas appointed elders in the early church.</li>
<li>The early church was ruled by elders, or bishops, and served by a community of deacons under the leadership of the apostles.</li>
<li>A three-fold structure of bishops, elders, and deacons developed; one bishop per congregation with multiple elders and deacons/deaconesses.</li>
<li>The power of the bishop grew; Rome was "first among equals" but bishops began to assert supremacy.</li>
<li>The office of deaconesses began to decline, disappearing by the Middle Ages.</li>
<li>Bishops in Rome, Alexandria, Antioch, Constantinople, and Jerusalem gain prestige, known as patriarchs; Constantinople second to Rome.</li>
<li>Leo I claimed authority over all bishops and the church based on primacy of the apostle Peter.</li>
</ul>
