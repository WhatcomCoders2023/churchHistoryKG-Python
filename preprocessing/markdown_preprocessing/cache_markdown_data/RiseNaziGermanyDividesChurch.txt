<h1>The Rise of Nazi Germany Divides the Church</h1>
<ul>
<li><strong>Period:</strong> !!smallcaps|ad!! 1919–1945</li>
<li><strong>Description:</strong> As the world entered another war, the church was divided over its response to Nazism.</li>
</ul>
<h2>Key Developments</h2>
<!--- A bulleted list of important developments during that period.-->

<ul>
<li>The desire for national unity after World War I lead to the rise in nationalism.</li>
<li>The rise of Nazi Germany led to World War II and the horrors of the Holocaust.</li>
<li>The German Christian movement aligned itself with Nazism; Protestant churches were unified into the Reich Church.</li>
<li>Nazism eventually attempted to position itself as a replacement of the Christian church.</li>
<li>German Protestants opposed to Nazism established the Confessing Church movement.</li>
</ul>

<h3>Neo-Orthodox Theologians</h3>
<ul>
<li><a href="https://ref.ly/logos4/Factbook?ref=bk.%25KarlBarth_Person">Karl Barth</a> (May 10, 1886–December 10, 1968): Swiss Protestant theologian; studied at Berlin, Tübingen, and Marburg; taught at Göttingen and Münster; opposed Protestant Liberalism; founded the Neo-Orthodoxy movement with Brunner; adhered to dialectical theology; advocated for a strong relationship between ethics and theology; opposed to Nazism; leader in the Confessing Church movement; author of <em>The Epistle to the Romans</em> ..., and <em>Church Dogmatics</em> </li>
<li><a href="https://ref.ly/logos4/Factbook?ref=bio.DietrichBonhoeffer">Dietrich Bonhoeffer</a> (February 4, 1906–April 9, 1945): German theologian and pastor; influenced by Barth; opposed Nazism; leader in the Confessing Church movement; executed for participating in assassination plot against Hitler during WWII; </li>
</ul>
<h3>Church Leaders</h3>
<ul>
<li><a href="https://ref.ly/logos4/Factbook?ref=bk.%25PopePiusXI_Person">Pope Pius XI</a> (1857–1939): pope of Rome; signed concordat with Hitler guaranteeing freedom to practice Catholicism in Germany.</li>
<li><a href="https://ref.ly/logos4/Factbook?ref=bk.%25LudwigMuller_Person">Ludwig Müller</a> (1883–1945): German Reichsbischof; bishop of Prussia; adviser to Adolf Hitler; supported Nazism; author of a Germanized version of the Sermon on the Mount.</li>
<li><a href="https://ref.ly/logos4/Factbook?ref=bk.%25MartinNiemoller_Person">Martin Niemöller</a> (January 14, 1892–March 6, 1984): German Lutheran theologian and pastor; leader of the Confession Church movement; arrested and imprisoned in a concentration camp until the end of WWII.</li>
</ul>
